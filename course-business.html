1. Common Business Terms

Essential

These fundamental terms form the backbone of professional business communication across all industries.

Example: "Our company's revenue increased by 15% this quarter due to higher sales volume."

Example: "After deducting all operating costs, our net profit was $250,000 last fiscal year."

Example: "Let's schedule a meeting next week to review the project timeline and deliverables."

Example: "The deadline for submitting the proposal is Friday at 5 PM—no extensions will be granted."

2. Business Roles & Positions

Organizational

Understanding organizational structures and job titles is crucial for professional communication and networking.

The highest-ranking executive in a company, responsible for making major corporate decisions and managing overall operations.

Example: "The CEO announced a new strategic direction for the company during the annual meeting."

An individual who is responsible for supervising a team or department, making decisions, and ensuring goals are met.

Example: "As a project manager, I oversee timelines, budgets, and team coordination."

A student or trainee who works, sometimes without pay, to gain work experience or satisfy requirements for a qualification.

Example: "We're hiring three summer interns to assist with market research and data analysis."

A professional who performs accounting functions such as audits, financial statement analysis, and tax preparation.

Example: "Our accountant prepares monthly financial reports and handles all tax filings."

3. Financial Vocabulary

Numbers

Key financial terms that every professional should know for budgeting, reporting, and strategic planning.

A time-stamped commercial document that itemizes and records a transaction between a buyer and seller.

Example: "Please send the invoice to accounting@company.com for payment processing."

An estimate of income and expenditure for a set period of time, used for planning and control purposes.

Example: "The marketing department needs to stay within its allocated budget for the quarter."

The cost required for something; the money spent on something to conduct business operations.

Example: "All travel expenses must be approved in advance and documented with receipts."

4. Office Communication Phrases

Conversational

Common expressions used in workplace conversations, meetings, and casual professional interactions.

"Let's touch base later."

Meaning: Suggests checking in or following up on a topic at a later time.

Usage: "I'm in meetings all morning—let's touch base after lunch to discuss the client's feedback."

"Can you follow up on that?"

Meaning: A request to take additional action or gather more information about a specific matter.

Usage: "The client asked about delivery timelines—can you follow up with logistics and get back to them?"

"I'll loop you in on the email."

Meaning: Indicates the speaker will include the listener in an email conversation for awareness or input.

Usage: "I'm emailing the vendor about the contract terms—I'll loop you in so you can review the details."

"Let's take this offline."

Meaning: Suggests discussing a topic separately, often after a meeting or outside the current conversation.

Usage: "This is an important issue—let's take it offline and schedule a separate meeting to address it properly."

5. Business Email Phrases

Written

Professional email expressions that convey politeness, clarity, and proper business etiquette.

"I hope this email finds you well."

A polite opening phrase used to start professional emails, showing consideration for the recipient.

Usage: "I hope this email finds you well. I'm writing to follow up on our meeting last Tuesday..."

"Please let me know if you have any questions."

A helpful closing phrase that invites further communication and shows willingness to assist.

Usage: "Attached is the report you requested. Please let me know if you have any questions or need additional information."

"Looking forward to your response."

A professional way to indicate you're awaiting a reply, often used to close emails that require action.

Usage: "Could you please confirm your availability by Friday? Looking forward to your response."

"Attached is the report for your review."

A clear statement indicating that documents are included with the email for examination.

Usage: "Attached is the report for your review. Please provide feedback by the end of the week."
