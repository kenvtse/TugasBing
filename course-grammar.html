1. Parts of Speech

Understanding the basic building blocks of English language and how they function in sentences.

  • Nouns: person, place, or thing (e.g., cat, school, happiness)
  • Verbs: action or state (e.g., run, be, think)
  • Adjectives: describe nouns (e.g., happy, fast, intelligent)
  • Adverbs: describe verbs/adjectives (e.g., quickly, very, extremely)
  • Pronouns: replace nouns (e.g., he, she, they)
  • Prepositions, Conjunctions, Interjections

2. Sentence Structure

Learn how to construct proper English sentences with correct word order and relationships.

  • Basic Pattern: Subject + Verb + Object
  • Example: She (subject) eats (verb) apples (object).
  • Compound Sentences: Using conjunctions (and, but, or)
  • Complex Sentences: Independent + dependent clauses
  • Four Types of Sentences: Declarative, Interrogative, Imperative, Exclamatory

3. Articles

Master the use of articles to specify or generalize nouns in your sentences.

Definite Article (the)

  • Used with specific nouns
  • Example: The sun is bright today.
  • Example: Please pass the salt.

Indefinite Articles (a/an)

  • Used with general, singular nouns
  • Example: I need a pen. (any pen)
  • Example: She's an engineer. (one of many)

4. Prepositions

Learn how prepositions show relationships between words in time, space, and logic.

Time Prepositions

  • at, in, on, during
  • Example: At 5pm, on Monday, in July

Place Prepositions

  • in, on, at, under, beside
  • Example: The book is on the table.

Direction Prepositions

  • to, from, into, towards
  • Example: She walked to the store.

5. Subject-Verb Agreement

Ensure your subjects and verbs always match in number and person.

Basic Rules

  • Singular subjects take singular verbs
  • Plural subjects take plural verbs
  • Correct: He runs. They run.
  • Incorrect: He run. They runs.

Special Cases

  • Collective nouns (team, family)
  • Indefinite pronouns (everyone, each)
  • Compound subjects (and, or, nor)
